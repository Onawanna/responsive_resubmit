Eli Gardiner

Eli was born and raised in Northern Michigan but now calls Minneapolis home. He learned to play guitar on his Dad’s old nylon string classical guitar and has been writing songs for over 15 years. With influences like Jason Isbell, John Moreland, and Bob Dylan, he values the purity of expression through his songs.

"When big changes have happened in my life music helps to make sense of it. I grew up with music, listening to “Saturday Night Gold” on the radio and my Dad’s albums of Jim Croce and Bruce Springsteen. So when my brother passed away from a drug overdose I turned to the one thing that helped to release some of that pain, hurt, and anger, writing songs."

"Through it all, Gardiner remains solemnly soft spoken, approaching questions of mortality with quiet care. A dour aura surrounds his stories, but so does a kind of peace found in their telling."-The All Scene Eye

"Eli's original sound and artistic creations will bring even the harshest music critic to their feet and having you begging for more! A MUST SEE artist!"-Joseph Bishop, England Street Tavern
